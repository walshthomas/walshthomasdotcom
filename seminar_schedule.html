Applied Economics
"Inequality and Crime: Evidence from French Prisons"
Dr Arnaud Philippe
Associate Professor · University of Bristol
Thursday, 26 February · 15:00
Room 102, Clarice Pears Building

Measuring the effect of inequality on crime is challenging — policies affect both simultaneously and individuals sort into neighbourhoods. Prison provides a rare natural experiment: inequality changes rapidly and inmates cannot move freely. Using rich French data on inmate localisation and misbehaviour, preliminary results show that higher inequality increases conflicts.
